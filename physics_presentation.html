How Membrane Breakout Physics Works

A Conceptual, Easy-to-Understand Guide to Our Fixed Constraints & Bugs

1. Core Concept: The Trampoline Paddle

Instead of hitting the ball with a flat, rigid paddle, Membrane Breakout 3D lets you bounce the ball on a flexible, circular elastic trampoline.

To make the game run smoothly at 60 FPS without slowing down your computer, the physics engine uses a smart, mathematical formula to instantly calculate the exact shape of the trampoline on every frame when the ball presses into it.

Physics in Action

  • The Stretch: Pressing the ball down stretches the trampoline, storing potential energy.
  • The Rebound: The trampoline pushes the ball back up with a vertical force proportional to the stretch depth.

Why It's Smooth

  • Zero Lag: No complex, slow simulation loops.
  • Perfect Integration: Computes ball movement in 20 tiny, ultra-stable sub-steps per frame.

2. Off-Center Bounces & Lateral Momentum

If you drop a ball onto a trampoline off-center, the trampoline surface becomes asymmetric (steeper on the side closer to the boundary). In the real world, this asymmetry pushes the ball sideways.

The Old Problem

  • No Sideways Push: The old physics only calculated vertical bounce forces.
  • Unnatural Drifting: If the ball hit off-center, it kept drifting sideways at a completely constant speed, ignoring the trampoline's slope.

The Fixed Solution

  • Stiffness Scaling: We updated the math to make the trampoline stiffer near the boundary.
  • Coupled Sideways Force: The ball now experiences a central restoring force pulling it sideways back toward the center of the paddle on off-center impacts.

3. Seamless Trampoline Mesh (Zero Tearing)

The mathematical shortcut we use to solve off-center shapes (Möbius mapping) stretches and distorts coordinates. This distortion introduced a physical error on the screen.

The Old Problem

  • Geometric Tearing: The sphere and the outer membrane did not meet cleanly at the boundary.
  • A Visual Gap: There was a non-physical gap/tear of up to 6.6 centimeters, making the trampoline look broken.

The Fixed Solution

  • Smooth Conformal Blending: We developed a direction-aware blending formula.
  • Seamless Connection: It calculates the mismatch and blends it smoothly to zero at the outer frame, making the trampoline mesh perfectly continuous with the sphere (0.00m gap).

4. Calibrated Cylinder & Paddle Clamping

The ball is unconstrained above and is pulled down by gravity, but it is bounded laterally by a circular wall (cylinder).

The "Zero-Loss" Geometry

  • Radius Calibrated to 1.45m: By setting the wall's radius to exactly 1.45m, the gap between the paddle and the wall is always smaller than the ball's diameter (1.0m).
  • Never Falls Below: The ball is geometrically trapped above the paddle, making the breakout loop endless and watertight!

Rigid-Body & Paddle Clamping

  • No Wall Penetration: Collisions trigger exactly when the ball's outer edge touches the wall, preventing visual clipping.
  • Moving inside the Wall: The paddle frame is clamped to stay strictly inside the cylinder. It slides smoothly along the cylinder's inner perimeter.

5. Summary of Fixed Conceptual Bugs

We successfully resolved the following major issues in the game engine to ensure natural, playable physics:

Bugs Discovered

  • No Sideways Bounce: Ball ignored trampoline slope and drifted constantly.
  • Tearing Membrane: A 6.6cm visual gap existed at the contact perimeter.
  • "Sticky" Ring: Ball got glued to the outer frame ring after contact.
  • Wall Penetration: Ball clipped through the outer cylinder by 50cm.
  • Timing Jitters: Fluctuating frame rates caused jerky, strange looking gravity.

Implemented Solutions

  • Coupled Restoring Forces: Restored coupled sideways force to rotate momentum naturally.
  • Smooth Height Blending: Eliminated the visual gap, making the mesh 100% seamless.
  • Dynamic Projections: Moved clamping inside active collision checks, enabling clean bounces.
  • Ball Edge Collisions: Calibrated checks to trigger exactly at the ball's outer edge.
  • Locked 60 FPS Physics: Set a deterministic physics time step to make gravity buttery-smooth.
